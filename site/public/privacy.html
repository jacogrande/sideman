Privacy Policy

Effective February 25, 2026 · Last updated February 25, 2026

Sideman is a macOS menu bar application that surfaces music credits and generates playlists. It is built and operated by Jackson (“we,” “us,” or “our”). This policy describes what data Sideman handles, how it is used, and your choices.

The short version

The Sideman app runs entirely on your Mac. We do not operate app servers, and the app does not collect analytics or track you. It reads your current Spotify playback locally, queries public music databases for credits, and uses the Spotify Web API only to create playlists with your explicit permission. Our website (sideman.app) uses privacy-friendly, cookie-free analytics to measure page views — see details below.

Playback detection

Sideman detects the currently playing track using macOS AppleScript, a local system call to the Spotify desktop app. This happens entirely on your Mac. The track title, artist, album, and Spotify track ID are read from the local Spotify process. This information is never transmitted to Sideman servers (none exist) and is used only to look up credits from third-party music databases.

Spotify authorization

To create playlists, Sideman requests your permission via Spotify’s standard OAuth 2.0 flow using PKCE (Proof Key for Code Exchange). The only permissions requested are:

  • playlist-modify-public — create and edit public playlists
  • playlist-modify-private — create and edit private playlists

Sideman does not request access to your listening history, saved library, user profile, or any other Spotify data. OAuth tokens are stored in your macOS Keychain with kSecAttrAccessibleWhenUnlockedThisDeviceOnly protection and are never transmitted beyond the Spotify API. You can revoke access at any time from your Spotify account settings.

Local data storage

Sideman stores two cache files on your Mac to avoid redundant network requests:

  • credits-cache.json — cached credit lookups, auto-expiring
  • discography-cache.json — cached artist discographies, expires after 7 days

Both files are stored in ~/Library/Application Support/sideman/ and contain only music metadata (track titles, artist names, roles, MusicBrainz IDs). They do not contain personal information. Uninstalling Sideman removes these files.

What we do not collect

  • No analytics, telemetry, or crash reporting in the app (website uses cookie-free Vercel Analytics)
  • No listening history or playback logs
  • No user accounts, emails, or personal profiles
  • No device identifiers, IP addresses, or location data
  • No third-party SDKs or tracking libraries
  • No advertising or marketing data

Sideman has zero external dependencies. The app is built entirely with Swift standard libraries and Apple frameworks.

Website analytics

The Sideman website (sideman.app) uses Vercel Analytics to collect anonymous, aggregated usage data such as page views, referrer URLs, and country of origin. Vercel Analytics does not use cookies, does not track individual users across sites, and does not collect personal information such as IP addresses or device identifiers. We also track anonymous click events on download buttons to understand interest. No data from the website is linked to your use of the Sideman app. You can review Vercel’s Analytics privacy policy for details.

Children’s privacy

Sideman does not knowingly collect any personal information from anyone, including children under 13. Since no personal data is collected or transmitted to us, there is no data to manage in this regard.

Data attribution

Music credits displayed in Sideman are sourced from MusicBrainz (core data licensed under CC0) and Wikipedia (content licensed under CC BY-SA). Popularity data is provided by ListenBrainz.

Changes to this policy

If we make material changes to this policy, we will update the date at the top of this page. Since Sideman does not collect contact information, this page is the sole notification channel for policy changes.
